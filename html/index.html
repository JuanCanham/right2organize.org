Berkeley Tenant Protections and Right to Organize is a ballot measure that will update Berkeley's Tenant protection ordinance to:

  • Reduce the scope and abuse of exemptions to Berkeley's Tenant protection ordinance
  • Expand ability of the city to enforce it's rental registry program
  • Ensure tenants are aware of their rights during eviction proceedings
  • Protect the rights of tenants to form tenants associations and discuss issues with their landlords in good faith
  • Other amendments and improvements to the language in the ordinance that can be found here

Major Donors: None


Corporate Landlords Submit Ballot Measure to Create Confusion for November 2024 Ballot

While Berkeley continues to face an extreme housing affordability crisis, two corporate landlords (Milestone Properties and Flynn Clement Properties) are trying to prevent Berkeley from protecting tenants. On March 21st, a notice was circulated for a ballot measure that would substantially weaken the ability of the Berkeley Rent Board to protect tenants in Berkeley. This comes in response to our coalition of unions, tenants, and community organizations coming together to collect signatures to strengthen Berkeley’s rent control ordinance with the Berkeley Tenant Protection and Right to Organize Act.

Confusion has been the most effective tool of corporate landlords in preventing protections for tenants from being passed in other cities. Let’s be clear - this measure is an attempt by corporate landlords to confuse Berkeley voters. Their measure would:

  • Prohibit the Rent Board from protecting tenants facing uninhabitable and unsafe conditions
  • Increase the amount rent can go up every year in rent controlled units
  • Prevents the Rent Board from suing landlords who violate the law
  • Strip tenants who currently have tenant rights from being protected by the Rent Board
  • Prevents reductions in rent when construction work seriously disrupts the ability of tenants to live in a unit, thereby incentivizing landlords to harass rent-controlled tenants into leaving through “renovictions”
  • Allows tenants to be evicted for violating any city ordinance, even if unrelated to their housing

In contrast, the Berkeley Tenant Protection and Right to Organize Act - which was publicly developed by the Berkeley Rent Board at numerous public meetings - will expand tenant protections and give tenants a robust and enforceable right to organize tenant associations.

Key Differences between the Tenant Measure and the Landlord Measure

Protection and Right to Organize Act (Tenant Measure) Corporate Landlord Measure
• Expands tenant protections to cover more tenants. • Removes many existing tenant protections.
• Reduces rent increases. • Larger rent increases.
• Right to organize a tenants union with a majority of tenants. • Reduces habitability protections

Leah Simon-Weisberg, Chair of the Berkeley Rent Board, said the following about this attempt to confuse voters:

I am confident that Berkeley voters will see through the corporate landlords’ strategy of chaos. Time and again, corporate money flows into Berkeley elections, only to be defeated by community organizing and the grassroots. The misinformation of these corporate landlords highlights the critical role of rent boards and tenant protections in keeping tenants in their homes.

Soli Alpert, Vice Chair of the Berkeley Rent Board, said,

We are disappointed but not surprised that rather than making their case to the voters on their opposition to tenant protections, the landlords and wealthy special interests are presenting their assault on tenants’ rights as a good thing for tenants in order to confuse the public. Only one measure, the Berkeley Tenant Protection and Right to Organize Act, will bring our rent control ordinance up to 21st century standards and keep tenants in their homes. By contrast, this corporate measure will weaken the Rent Board’s ability to protect tenants and strip tenants of protections they currently have.”
